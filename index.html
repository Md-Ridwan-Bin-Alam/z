Md. Ridwan Bin Alam

Md. Ridwan Bin Alam is right now working as a lecturer at Civil Engineering department of Bangladesh Army International University of Science and Technology (BAIUST), Comilla Cantonment, Bangladesh. He has completed B.Sc in Civil Engineering from Ahsanullah University of Science & Technology, Dhaka, Bangladesh. He also attended Notredame College, Dhaka and Chittagong Collegiate school, Chittagong to complete Higher Secondary Certificate and Secondary School Certificate examination.

He has done his undergraduate thesis on Differential Settlement at Bridge Approaches: Bump at the end of the bridge . His research interests includes Sustainable Construction Material, Fire Modeling, Fire safe Structural Design, Architectural Considerations for Fire Safety and Construction Materials Behavior in Fire etc . He is a member of the Institution of Engineers, Bangladesh (IEB).

He wants to enrich his knowledge through working in a challenging environment where he can utilize his education and skills. He has a strong desire to work with the best research teams in the world so that he can build up a strong career in academia and establish himself as a researcher.

Technical Skills

fds-smv (Fire Dynamics simulator and Smokeview):

Fire dynamics simulator (FDS) is a computational fluid dynamics (CFD) model of fire-driven fluid flow. The software solves numerically a large eddy simulation form of the Navier Stokes equations appropriate for low-speed, thermally-driven flow, with an emphasis on smoke and heat transport from fires. FDS is free software developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) of the United States Department of Commerce, in cooperation with VTT Technical Research Centre of Finland. Smokeview is the companion visualization program that can be used to display the output of FDS.

CFI Calculator: CFI Calculator is a software that allows you to calculate different elements when a fire is starting, taking in consideration various factors that can amplify the effects of the fire, such as the dimension of a room, electricity, wind , the heat flux or heat release and much more.

ABAQUS: Abaqus is a software suite for finite element analysis and computer-aided engineering, originally released in 1978. The name and logo of this software are based on the abacus calculation tool. The Abaqus product suite consists of five core software products.

LS-DYNA: LS-DYNA is an advanced general-purpose multiphysics simulation software package developed by the Livermore Software Technology Corporation (LSTC). While the package continues to contain more and more possibilities for the calculation of many complex, real world problems, its origins and core-competency lie in highly nonlinear transient dynamic finite element analysis (FEA) using explicit time integration. LS-DYNA is being used by the automobile, aerospace, construction, military, manufacturing, and bioengineering industries.
